Desiccant Paper

This highly absorbant material works great for pressing flowers. Create a sandwich of desiccant paper, viva paper towels, flowers and botanicals, viva paper towels and repeat. You can have several layers / sandwiches in a stack. I use pieces of old paneling cut to size for the top and bottom of the stack to protect the desiccant paper. Cover the stack with 1-2 patio bricks. Change the desiccant paper every 1-2 days until the botanicals are completely dry.

Desiccant paper can be recharged in the oven to remove the moisture. I set my oven at the lowest heat (170 degrees) and recharge 10 sheets to a shelf for 20+ minutes. Then store the sheets in a mylar or plastic bag until needed.

You can purchase desiccant paper from www.sorbentsystems.com
Iron On Vinyl Laminate

I use drytac brand laminate to protect the flowers on my greeting cards, journals and related products. It has tiny holes which are sealed by heat. You can either use a warm iron with a piece of cloth between the iron and the surface of the card, or you can use a laminator. When using a laminator be sure to place the card in a carrier (such as a manilla folder cut to size).

Ordering information follows: Artshield Mattex UV 25.5 inches wide purchased from www.drytac.com

You can purchase it in "satin" or "gloss".
